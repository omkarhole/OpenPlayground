The Science Behind the Forgetting Curve

The forgetting curve was first described by German psychologist Hermann Ebbinghaus in 1885. He discovered that memory retention declines exponentially over time if no attempt is made to retain the information.

Key findings from Ebbinghaus's research:

  • We forget approximately 56% of information after one hour
  • 66% is forgotten after one day
  • 75% is forgotten after six days
  • Without review, only about 20-30% remains after a month

How to Combat the Forgetting Curve

Research shows that spaced repetition is the most effective way to flatten the forgetting curve and improve long-term memory retention.

Effective strategies include:

  • Review shortly after learning (within 20-60 minutes)
  • Schedule follow-up reviews at increasing intervals
  • Use active recall instead of passive re-reading
  • Interleave different subjects to strengthen memory
  • Teach the material to someone else
