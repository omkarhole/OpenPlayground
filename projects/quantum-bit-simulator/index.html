What is a Qubit?

A qubit is the quantum analog of a classical bit. Unlike a bit, which can be 0 or 1, a qubit can be in a superposition of both states simultaneously.

Superposition

Superposition allows a qubit to exist in multiple states at once. For example, a qubit can be 70% |0⟩ and 30% |1⟩ until measured.

Quantum Gates

Gates like Hadamard create superposition, while Pauli gates (X, Y, Z) manipulate the qubit's state.

Measurement

Measuring a qubit collapses its superposition to a definite state (0 or 1) with probabilities based on the amplitudes.

Entanglement

Entanglement links qubits so the state of one instantly influences the other, regardless of distance.

Quantum Random Number Generation

Using superposition and measurement, we can generate truly random numbers, unlike classical methods.
