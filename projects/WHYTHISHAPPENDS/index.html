Why is the sky blue?
The science of sunlight scattering in Earth's atmosphere
The Short Answer

The sky appears blue because of a phenomenon called Rayleigh scattering. Sunlight reaches Earth's atmosphere and is scattered in all directions by gases and particles in the air. Blue light is scattered more than other colors because it travels as shorter, smaller waves.

How Sunlight Scatters in the Atmosphere

Sunlight entering Earth's atmosphere gets scattered by air molecules

Rayleigh Scattering

Named after British physicist Lord Rayleigh, this is the elastic scattering of light by particles much smaller than the wavelength of light.

Color Spectrum

Sunlight contains all colors of the rainbow. Blue light has a shorter wavelength (about 475 nm) compared to red light (about 650 nm).

Sunset Colors

At sunset, light travels through more atmosphere, scattering blue light away and leaving reds and oranges visible.

Fun Fact

If Earth had no atmosphere, the sky would appear black even during the day, just like it does on the Moon! Astronauts on the Moon see a black sky because there's no atmosphere to scatter sunlight.

Why do we dream?
The mysterious world of sleep and brain activity
The Science of Dreams

Dreams are stories and images our minds create while we sleep. They can be vivid, emotional, bizarre, or completely ordinary. While scientists still don't fully understand why we dream, several theories suggest dreams help with memory processing, emotional regulation, and problem-solving.

Memory Processing

During REM sleep, the brain organizes and stores memories from the day, strengthening important neural connections.

Emotional Processing

Dreams may help process emotions, allowing the brain to work through challenging feelings in a safe environment.

Problem Solving

Some theories suggest dreams help solve problems by allowing the brain to make connections it might not make while awake.

Sleep Cycles & Dreaming

Dreams occur most frequently and vividly during REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep, which happens about 90 minutes after falling asleep. During REM sleep, brain activity increases to levels similar to being awake, but the body's muscles are temporarily paralyzed (to prevent acting out dreams).

Dream Facts

• People typically have 3-5 dreams per night, but forget 95-99% of them upon waking
• Everyone dreams, even if they don't remember
• Blind people dream too - their dreams involve other senses like sound, touch, and smell
• Animals also experience REM sleep and likely dream

Why do earthquakes happen?
The shifting tectonic plates beneath our feet
Plate Tectonics

Earthquakes occur when two blocks of the earth suddenly slip past one another. The surface where they slip is called the fault or fault plane. The location below the earth's surface where the earthquake starts is called the hypocenter, and the location directly above it on the surface is called the epicenter.

Tectonic Plate Movement

→

Tectonic plates moving against each other create friction that eventually releases as seismic waves

Earth's Structure

The Earth's outer layer is divided into several tectonic plates that float on the semi-fluid asthenosphere beneath them.

Seismic Waves

When plates slip, energy is released in the form of seismic waves that travel through the Earth, causing the ground to shake.

Ring of Fire

About 90% of earthquakes occur along the "Ring of Fire," a path along the Pacific Ocean with high volcanic and seismic activity.

Earthquake Scale

The Richter scale measures earthquake magnitude logarithmically. A magnitude 6.0 earthquake releases about 32 times more energy than a magnitude 5.0 earthquake, and about 1,000 times more energy than a magnitude 4.0 earthquake!
