Brain
Control Center of the Body
Nervous System

The brain is the most complex organ in the human body, responsible for thoughts, memory, emotions, touch, motor skills, vision, breathing, temperature, and every process that regulates our body.

Common Diseases & Prevention
Alzheimer's & Dementia

Progressive brain disorders that affect memory, thinking, and behavior. Risk increases with age but is not a normal part of aging.

Prevention Tip: Regular mental stimulation, social engagement, and physical activity can help maintain brain health.
Stroke

Occurs when blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients.

Prevention Tip: Control blood pressure, maintain healthy cholesterol levels, avoid smoking, and exercise regularly.
Health Tips for Brain Wellness
Stay Mentally Active

Puzzles, reading, and learning new skills can help build cognitive reserve.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Found in fish, nuts, and seeds, these support brain cell structure and function.

Quality Sleep

7-9 hours nightly helps clear brain toxins and consolidate memories.

Stress Management

Chronic stress can damage brain cells; meditation and yoga can help.

Heart
Your Lifelong Pump
Cardiovascular System

The heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the body via the circulatory system, supplying oxygen and nutrients to tissues and removing carbon dioxide and other wastes.

Common Diseases & Prevention
Coronary Artery Disease

The most common type of heart disease, caused by plaque buildup in the walls of arteries that supply blood to the heart.

Prevention Tip: Eat a heart-healthy diet low in saturated fats, trans fats, and cholesterol.
Heart Attack

Occurs when blood flow to part of the heart is blocked, usually by a blood clot, damaging the heart muscle.

Prevention Tip: Regular physical activity strengthens the heart muscle and improves circulation.
Heart-Healthy Habits
Exercise Regularly

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week, like brisk walking or cycling.

Avoid Tobacco

Smoking damages blood vessels and increases heart disease risk. Quitting has immediate and long-term benefits.

Lungs
Your Breathing Powerhouse
Respiratory System

The lungs are a pair of spongy, air-filled organs located on either side of the chest. They bring oxygen into the body and remove carbon dioxide through the process of respiration.

Common Diseases & Prevention
Asthma

A condition in which airways narrow and swell, producing extra mucus, making breathing difficult.

Prevention Tip: Identify and avoid triggers like pollen, dust mites, or pet dander.
COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease)

A group of lung diseases that block airflow and make breathing difficult, often caused by long-term exposure to irritating gases or particulate matter.

Prevention Tip: The most important step is to quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke.
Lung Health Tips
Deep Breathing Exercises

Practice diaphragmatic breathing to increase lung capacity and efficiency.

Avoid Pollutants

Minimize exposure to air pollution, chemical fumes, and dust.

Stay Hydrated

Drinking enough water keeps the mucosal linings in the lungs thin.

Regular Exercise

Improves lung capacity and strengthens respiratory muscles.
