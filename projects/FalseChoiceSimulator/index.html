The Restaurant Menu
You're at a restaurant with a friend. The menu offers two options: a $25 steak or a $24 chicken dish. Both are priced similarly to make the steak seem like a better value, but cheaper options were intentionally omitted.

Choose the Steak ($25)

Premium cut with sides - appears as the better value

Choose the Chicken ($24)

Grilled chicken with similar sides - only $1 cheaper

Hidden Constraints Revealed

The False Choice: You were only shown two similarly-priced options.

Hidden Alternatives: The restaurant actually has 8 other dishes priced between $12-$18 that were not shown on your menu.

Psychological Bias: This is an example of Decoy Effect - presenting two options where one seems superior due to strategic comparison.

Real Choice: You could have asked for the full menu or chosen a different restaurant entirely.

About False Choices

False choices are decision scenarios where options are presented as free choices, but are actually constrained or manipulated to guide you toward a specific outcome.

Illusion of Choice

Presenting multiple options that all lead to the same outcome.

Framing Effect

How information is presented influences decisions more than the information itself.

Decoy Effect

Adding an inferior option to make another option seem more attractive.
